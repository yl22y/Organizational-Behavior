Welcome: What Are We Comparing?

In this mini lesson, you’ll learn how to distinguish between:

Routine & Adaptive Task Performance
Organizational Citizenship Behavior (OCB)
Voice Behavior

By the end, you should be able to look at a short scenario and identify: “Is this task performance, OCB, or voice?”

Task Performance

Task performance is about how well someone does the core duties formally required by their job.

Routine

Routine Task Performance

Basic, predictable job duties that follow established procedures.

  • Processing customer orders correctly
  • Showing up on time and prepared
  • Following safety and quality guidelines
Adaptive & Proactive

Adaptive / Proactive Task Performance

Handling change, solving problems, and taking initiative that is still part of “doing your job well.”

  • Learning a new system quickly
  • Re-prioritizing work when emergencies arise
  • Anticipating an issue and fixing it early

OCB: Going Beyond Formal Requirements

Organizational Citizenship Behaviors (OCBs) are discretionary, extra-role actions that help the social and organizational environment.

OCB-I

OCB toward Individuals

Helping specific coworkers with their work or well-being.

  • Staying late to help a teammate finish a report
  • Covering a shift so a coworker can go to an appointment
  • Informally mentoring a new hire
OCB-O

OCB toward the Organization

Actions that promote the organization as a whole.

  • Speaking positively about the company to outsiders
  • Volunteering for an internal committee or event
  • Following “unwritten rules” that support the culture

Voice: Speaking Up to Improve the Organization

Voice is a specific, higher-risk type of citizenship behavior: proactively speaking up with suggestions or concerns to improve the organization.

Promotive Voice

Suggesting Improvements

Offering ideas for new practices, products, or processes.

  • Proposing a new onboarding process to reduce errors
  • Recommending a better way to schedule shifts
Prohibitive Voice

Pointing Out Risks or Problems

Calling attention to practices that are inefficient, unsafe, or unfair.

  • Raising concerns about a safety violation
  • Alerting leadership to unethical sales practices

Practice: What Kind of Behavior Is This?

Scenario
Jordan notices that customers are often confused by the new return policy. After their shift, Jordan stays late (unpaid) to create a simple one-page guide and emails it to the whole team and manager.

Is this mainly task performance, OCB-I, OCB-O, or voice?

Suggested coding:
This is primarily OCB-O (going beyond formal duties to help the organization as a whole by improving a process). If Jordan also speaks up in a meeting to propose adopting the guide as a standard process, that part would count as promotive voice.

In class, you can ask students to generate their own examples and label them as task performance, OCB-I, OCB-O, or voice.
